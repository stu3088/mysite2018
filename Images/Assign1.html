However, while copying nature in biomimetics or biomimicry, as it is known, as resulted in breakthroughs in the lab, the challenge will be taking the prototypes and manufacturing them at scale. In addition to imitating natural materials, work is also being done to replicate the function of human tissues. At Oxford university, for example, Vanessa Restrepo-Schild, a doctoral student and researcher, is leading the development of a synthetic retina that mimics natural human retinal processes. The synthetic retina consists of a cell membrane containing aqueous droplets. “Inside those aqueous droplets we added the minimal biological components necessary to detect light and generate an electrical signal,” she explains.
